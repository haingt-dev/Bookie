# Author: James Clear

## Background
- Blogger → best-selling author. Built massive audience through newsletter (jamesclear.com) before publishing Atomic Habits.
- Personal story: baseball injury (bat struck face, fractured skull, brain displaced). Recovery journey inspired habits framework.
- Influenced by: Charles Duhigg (habit loop), BJ Fogg (tiny habits), behavioral psychology.

## Core Thesis
Small changes + right systems + identity shift = transformation. The compound effect of daily habits outweighs dramatic one-time efforts. Identity ("who you are") drives behavior more reliably than motivation or willpower.

## Strengths
- Accessible frameworks that non-academics can apply immediately
- Compelling stories with visual potential (British Cycling, Diderot Effect, Laszlo Polgar chess experiment)
- Practical tools: habit stacking, environment design, 2-minute rule, never miss twice

## Blind Spots (identified in Bookie research)
1. **37x math is metaphorical, not predictive** — humans don't compound like bank accounts. Diminishing returns, plateaus, sudden jumps.
2. **2-minute rule can misfire** — teaches HOW to build habits, not WHICH habits to choose. Forcing a habit you hate via a trick = eventual burnout.
3. **Systems without goals plateau** — "showing up" without direction leads to stagnation. Contradicts Peter Thiel's emphasis on bold singular goals.
