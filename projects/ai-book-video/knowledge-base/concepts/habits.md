# Theme: Thói Quen (Habits)

> Tất cả concepts liên quan đến xây dựng, duy trì, và phá vỡ thói quen — xuyên suốt mọi cuốn sách.

## Concepts

### Compound interest of self-improvement
- **Source**: Atomic Habits (James Clear)
- **Position**: 1% better daily = 37x better yearly. Habits are the "compound interest" of self-improvement.
- **Nuance**: Metaphor, not math. Humans experience diminishing returns — gym 0→1h = massive gain, 1h→2h = marginal. Real progress = plateaus + sudden jumps, not smooth exponential curves. (Dr. Gina Cleo, medical science research)
- **Cross-ref**: → connections/contradictions.md #clear-vs-thiel

### Systems over Goals
- **Source**: Atomic Habits (James Clear)
- **Position**: "You fall to the level of your systems." Goals = direction, systems = daily processes that create results.
- **Nuance**: True for consistency, but directionless systems plateau indefinitely. Gym 5 years, same routine, zero progress. Systems need goals as compass, not competitor.
- **Cross-ref**: → connections/contradictions.md #clear-vs-thiel

### Gateway habits / 2-minute rule
- **Source**: Atomic Habits (James Clear)
- **Position**: Start any habit in under 2 minutes. Lower activation energy to make starting effortless.
- **Nuance**: Can misfire if the habit doesn't align with identity. Using a trick to force gym attendance → hate every second → quit → self-blame. Book teaches HOW to build habits, not WHICH habits to choose.

### Four Laws of Behavior Change
- **Source**: Atomic Habits (James Clear)
- **Position**: Every habit = Cue → Craving → Response → Reward. Create good: Make it Obvious, Attractive, Easy, Satisfying. Break bad: reverse each.
- **Nuance**: Powerful framework but doesn't account for emotional/trauma-driven habits. Environment design (đặt vitamin cạnh vòi nước) works for simple cues, less for complex behavioral patterns.

### Habit stacking
- **Source**: Atomic Habits (James Clear)
- **Position**: Anchor new habits to existing neural pathways. "After [existing habit], I will [new habit]."
- **Nuance**: Leverages Diderot Effect — one change cascades into related changes. Effective when the anchor habit is truly automatic, less so when both habits require willpower.

### Environment design
- **Source**: Atomic Habits (James Clear)
- **Position**: "Environment is the invisible hand that shapes human behavior." Redesign physical space to make good habits obvious and bad habits invisible.
- **Nuance**: Powerful for physical cues. Less applicable to digital/social environments where cues are algorithmically designed to override personal choice.
